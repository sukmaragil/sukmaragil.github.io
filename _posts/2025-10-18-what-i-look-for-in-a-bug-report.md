I've filed and triaged a lot of bugs, and the difference between a report that gets fixed today and one that languishes is rarely the severity. It's the quality of the report.

Three things matter most. First, a reproduction: exact steps, exact environment, exact data. "Sometimes it's broken" is not a bug report, it's a feeling. Second, the gap between expected and actual — stated plainly, because the developer may not share your mental model of "correct."

Third, evidence: a log line, a screenshot, a failing request. Give the person who has to fix it a head start, and you'll be amazed how much faster the fix arrives. A bug report is a handoff, and good handoffs are an act of respect.
